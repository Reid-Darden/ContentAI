TaylorMade Stealth HD Driver

The Next Era of Speed, Distance, and Draw Bias

The new Stealth HD Driver by TaylorMade marks the end of the titanium era of club face design. Over 20 years of Research and Development have gone into the design of the new Stealth HD Driver, utilizing an abundance of new design techniques and materials in the process. The Stealth HD Driver also features a draw bias design, helping to promote a draw ball flight without sacrificing any forgiveness.

New 60X Carbon Twist Face

The new 60X Carbon Twist Face found on the TaylorMade Stealth HD Driver comes in at 44% lighter than other titanium face drivers while also being 11% larger in size than the previous SIM and SIM2 models. The introduction of 60 carbon fiber layers into the face is instrumental in delivering higher ball speeds across more surface area of the face at impact.

Nanotexture Cover Promotes High MOI with Draw Bias

The 60X Carbon Twist Face is housed under a newly created polyurethane cover that utilizes nanotexture technology on the new Stealth HD Driver. This cover optimizes spin and launch characteristics to maximize distance in all playing conditions. The weight that is saved with the combination of the 60X Carbon Twist Face and the Nanotexture cover allows the Stealth HD Driver to have a lower and heel-favoring CG, further boosting MOI and giving guaranteed draw bias on every swing.

Inertia Generator and Speed Slot Make a Return

The Stealth HD Driver returns a few of the game-changing technologies present from previous generations of TaylorMade Drivers. Firstly, the flexible Speed Pocket on the sole raises ball speed at impact, no matter the position of the strike on the face. The sole also features the Asymmetric Inertia Generator, a design characteristic found on both SIM and SIM2 that helps generate additional club head speed on the downswing, giving you more distance.
